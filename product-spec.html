Product spec — summary

Updated 2026-06-09 · A human-readable digest of 05-product-spec/ (functionality, UI, screens, data model, API, tech stack).
A merchant-acceptance app for the DRC: a merchant accepts payment from a customer on any mobile-money network (Vodacom M-Pesa, Airtel, Orange), with cross-network bridging handled behind the scenes. The customer pays the exact sticker price and needs no app — they pay by USSD / QR from any phone. It's a pass-through — we never hold the money; funds flow straight from the customer, through our payments partner (pawaPay), and settle instantly to the merchant's mobile-money account (net of our fee).
Subject to revision when the team grows. This reflects current decisions with the existing small team. New engineers will shape specifics (especially the backend language and framework choices). The durable parts: the merchant-acceptance flow, the customer-USSD / QR channel, merchant onboarding, the pass-through model, the coral-minimal design, and the data/API shape.

The core flow

Primary flow — customer-initiated USSD (no customer app):

Merchant sets the amount→ Customer scans the QR (a tel: USSD dial-through) or dials the till *123*1001#→ Customer enters their operator PIN on their own phone's prompt→ Collect + settle→ Merchant sees the payment + receipt

Fallback — merchant-initiated "charge by customer number": the merchant keys the customer's number to push a collection prompt to that customer's phone.

Behind the scenes it's two legs: collect from the customer's wallet, then settle (pay out) to the merchant's wallet — bridging networks when they differ. The customer pays the exact sticker price; the merchant absorbs our fee. If the settlement fails, the customer is automatically refunded.

UI & UX

Direction: Wave-style minimal (one primary action per screen, lots of whitespace) with a distinctive coral accent — warm and human, deliberately different from every operator's brand. The app is the merchant's tool; the customer never sees it.

Palette: Coral #FF6B5B Cream #FFFCF8 Charcoal #1A1A1A — plus green/red/amber for success/failure/warning. Light + dark mode (follows the phone setting).

  • Type: Inter (clean geometric sans; renders French accents well).
  • Components: soft + flat — 12px rounded corners, no shadows (cleaner + faster on low-cost Android).
  • Navigation: 3 tabs — Encaisser (Accept / get paid) · Historique (History) · Profil (Profile).
  • Language: French only in v1 (structured so Lingala/Swahili can be added later).
  • Tone: functional + warm; never blame the user; verbs on buttons.

Data model

Postgres. The core entities are Merchant, Transaction, LedgerEntry, with a banking-grade double-entry ledger (every movement recorded twice so the books always balance). The ledger accounts are customer:external, merchant:external, pawapay:clearing, and revenue:fees.

merchantsessionotp_attemptpayment_code / tilltransactionledger_entryoperator_eventoperator_statusaudit_log

Planned additions: merchant auth, a webhook receiver (Phase D), and reconciliation. There is no consumer User / Recipient model.

The transaction's life

initiated→ collection_pending→ collection_succeeded→ settlement_pending→ settlement_succeeded ✓

…with branches for collection_failed, settlement_failed → refund_pending → refunded (refund to the customer), and manual_review (the only state needing a human). Anything stuck self-heals via a reconciliation job. Detail: data-model.md.

API

REST + a USSD channel, JSON, JWT sessions, idempotency keys on every money-moving request. Endpoints span Merchant auth, Merchants, Payment codes / till, Transactions, System, plus a webhook receiver. Organised as domains/(ledger, merchants, transactions) → application/(start_merchant_payment, payment_codes) → channels(http/, ussd/).

  • Key endpoints: POST /transactions/preview (amount + MDR fee breakdown), POST /transactions (start a merchant payment), GET /transactions/{id} (status), GET /transactions (history), /merchants/* (onboarding, settlement, till / QR), /auth/otp/*.
  • USSD channel drives the customer-initiated dial-through (QR encodes a tel:*123*1001%23 string; the till is *123*1001#).
  • Webhooks from pawaPay are verified with RFC-9421 public-key signatures (ECDSA-P256) — not HMAC — by fetching pawaPay's public key. (This was a corrected assumption.)

Full contracts: api-contracts.md.

Performance & security targets

  • Mobile cold-start under 2s on a mid-range Android (Tecno/Infinix-class); crash-free sessions > 99.5%.
  • API p95 under 300ms (non-pawaPay endpoints); reconciliation lag under 1 hour at steady state.
  • Merchant app passcode hashed with Argon2id; secrets in AWS Secrets Manager; daily DB backups with tested restore.
  • Accessibility: WCAG AA contrast, 48dp touch targets, screen-reader labels.

Open decisions

  • MDR level — the merchant fee. 1% is a placeholder and is below the ~3.5–5% pawaPay round-trip cost; a viable MDR is likely ~5–7%+. This pricing decision is open and must be settled before the pilot.
  • Backend language — Python (FastAPI, as built) vs Node; the final call sits with the backend engineer.
  • Monetisation levers (from the comparables research) to fold in: MDR on acceptance (the core earner here), CDF↔USD FX spread, capital-light partner-bank lending later.
  • Legal/licensing posture (BCC) — the standing flag.
