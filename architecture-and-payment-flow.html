DRC Pay

How the system is built & how a payment flows

A Python/FastAPI backend runs the payment logic against a double-entry ledger in PostgreSQL and answers requests over a REST API, while pawaPay actually moves the mobile money — we stay a pure pass-through and never hold funds. The screens are plain web pages; the whole thing ships as a Docker container on Railway.

Infographic 2

Payment flow — scan to paid

One successful payment, end to end. The customer needs no app — they just scan the merchant's charge QR. Steps in green are where money actually moves and the books are written.

1
Customer scans the charge QR
QR → /customer/?charge=<id>
The QR encodes a link to the pay page for this exact charge. Their phone's browser opens it — nothing to install.
2
The pay page loads the charge
GET /public/charge/{id}
Shows the locked amount and the merchant. The amount is set by the server from the charge — never typed by the customer.
3
Customer picks their network & taps Pay
POST /pay
Their mobile-money operator (Vodacom / Airtel / Orange) plus the charge's amount go to the backend.
4
The backend starts the payment
start_merchant_payment → Orchestrator
Computes the fee from the network pair, creates the transaction, and kicks off the state machine.
5
Collect money in flight
pawaPay deposit
pawaPay debits the customer; the funds sit at pawaPay (a "clearing" holding spot). Nothing is settled yet.
6
Settle to the merchant books written
pawaPay payout · ledger postings
The merchant receives amount − fee; pawaPay's cost is booked to expense, our margin to revenue. Every posting balances.
7
Confirm — both screens go "Paid"
signed webhook (RFC-9421)
pawaPay confirms each leg with a signed callback; the Merchant Console and the customer's page both flip to Paid in real time.
↩ If the payout fails after collection — the auto-refund branch
The customer is automatically refunded the full amount. pawaPay already took its collection fee, so that is booked as a real loss (expense:pawapay) — visible on the books, never hidden. A refund that itself fails is escalated to manual_review. If the customer's callback never arrives, a reconciliation sweep polls pawaPay and heals it.

+ The seam where profit appears
Today the fee (MDR) equals pawaPay's cost, so revenue:fees is $0. Mark the MDR up — say to 6% = $0.60 — and the merchant nets $9.40, pawaPay still costs $0.45, and the extra $0.15 lands in revenue automatically. That one number is the pricing decision.
↩ What a refund looks like on the books
If the payout fails after collection, the customer is refunded the full $10.00 — but pawaPay already took its $0.25 collection fee, so that stays in expense:pawapay as a booked loss. The books always show exactly where every dollar went.
